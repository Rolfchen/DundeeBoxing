Why is it called "Boxing Confidence for Trainers"?

These training courses will provide specific processes to improve skill levels, as well as educating people about boxing in general. Learning proper boxing skills is fun and helps manage stress levels. You can even teach your family and friends!


Accredited Boxing Courses by Fitness Australia

Accredited Boxing Courses by Fitness Australia:

  • Accredited by Australia Fitness, Continual Education Credits (CEC 15 points)
  • Boxing Confidence for Trainer, Level 1: Beginner to Intermediate (CEC 8 points)
  • Boxing Confidence for Trainer, Level 2: Advanced (CEC 7 points)

Boxing is a proven exercise that is extremely popular for weight loss and fitness in today’s modern society. You can often see people boxing in a park or in gyms. Our trainers use correct boxing technique to offer a full body workout, helping clients lose weight and improve their fitness levels.

There are two different types of boxing: One for fitness and one for serious boxers. I want to talk about fitness boxing.

Fitness boxing involves people doing pad work without basic boxing skills, they just want to improve their fitness level and lose weight. They might want to keep it simple as they do not want to think too much after work.

My recommendation is that it is better to learn proper boxing skills if they want to improve their fitness levels.

It is more effective and efficient, and you will use more muscles and energy than people who just do pad work without proper boxing skills.

This boxing course improves your specific boxing skill, as well as improves your muscle groups such as calf, hamstring, back, arm and core muscles.

When you first learn it may be challenging, but gradually you see noticeable changes in speed, reaction times, coordination, tone of your muscles and confidence.

Aims and learning outcomes

1.Learn the fundamental skills and techniques of boxing from trained boxer.
2.Learn orthodox and unorthodox stance
3.Demonstrate correct posture and foot position
4.Demonstrate pivot and slide technique (circling)
5.Demonstrate side to side and back and forth rhythm
6.Identify the two types of boxer style
7.Perform basic defensive techniques
8.Competently wrap the hands
9.Perform jabs
10.Perform straight rights
11.Perform hooks

Assessment Method

All assessment is observational assessment against checklists which specifically relate to the course content, aims and learning outcomes of the program
